# Event Report: Saturday Meet up

### Overview
- **Event Name:** Saturday Meet up  
- **Date & Time:** 09:00 - 12:00, May 30, 2026  
- **Location:** Floor 26, Bitexco Financial Tower, No. 2 Hai Trieu Street, Ben Nghe Ward, District 1, Ho Chi Minh City  
- **Role:** Attendee (FCJ Intern / Frontend Developer for Aura Academic)  

---

### Event Introduction
Saturday Meet up felt vastly different from traditional university lectures or rigid corporate training sessions. Walking into the AWS office on the 26th floor of Bitexco Financial Tower, my first impression was the vibrant, open atmosphere filled with passionate cloud computing enthusiasts.

This meetup provided an invaluable opportunity for interns, industry speakers, and mentors to sit down together, share practical experiences, and discuss the real-world lessons learned when building scalable cloud applications. Rather than just listening passively, we were actively encouraged to ask tough questions and dissect technical roadblocks directly from our ongoing team projects.

---

### Presentation Topics & Real-World Project Reflections

Throughout the morning, 6 insightful presentations delivered fresh perspectives that closely mirrored the challenges our group faced while developing **Aura Academic**:

#### 1. Level up your AWS Skills with Cloud Quest and Floci
* **Speaker:** Huỳnh Thái Lĩnh *(First Cloud Journey - FCJ Program)*
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** Mr. Lĩnh introduced an engaging approach to mastering cloud competencies: **gamification** through AWS Cloud Quest and the Floci platform. Instead of getting bogged down by dry technical documentation or worrying about unexpected cloud bills while experimenting, solving gamified architectural puzzles in a simulated cloud city made concepts like VPC routing, EC2 sizing, and IAM policies much easier to internalize and apply.

#### 2. Hackathon Introduction – Team Collaboration & Product Strategy
* **Group:** The Ballers *(Huỳnh An Khương, Mai Quốc Anh, Nguyễn Trần Minh Quân)*
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** Hearing The Ballers recount their high-pressure Hackathon journeys highlighted the immense challenge of strict deadlines. They shared practical strategies for brainstorming, defining a tight Minimum Viable Product (MVP), and maintaining seamless team alignment. This immediately reminded me of our **Aura Academic** project: with a strict 12-week internship timeline, getting distracted by complex, non-essential features can easily derail the entire build. My biggest takeaway: *deliver fewer features, but ensure they run flawlessly with exceptional polish*.

#### 3. Why We Always Need Confidence
* **Speaker:** Nguyễn Thị Quỳnh Như
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** This soft-skills session resonated deeply with me. In software engineering, we often struggle with Imposter Syndrome—feeling inadequate when facing rapidly evolving technologies. Ms. Quỳnh Như delivered an inspiring message on building the confidence to speak up in technical discussions, actively seek guidance from mentors, and trust the genuine value we bring to our engineering teams.

#### 4. Case Study: Tử Vi Đại Việt Website
* **Website:** https://tuvidaiviet.com/
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** Examining a real-world product that successfully translated cultural concepts into a responsive web application was extremely eye-opening. I focused closely on their User Flow design and how they handled performance optimization when rendering complex astrological charts. As a Frontend Developer, I gained practical ideas on designing web interfaces that remain highly accessible while maintaining fast load times across mobile devices.

#### 5. The Hidden Iceberg of the Project: DevOps Before Disaster
* **Speaker:** Trần Minh Quân
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** Mr. Quân compared DevOps to the massive underwater portion of an iceberg—if neglected, it can trigger catastrophic system failures upon launch. He stressed that CI/CD pipelines, automated testing, and active monitoring must be integrated from the beginning, rather than treated as an afterthought. This advice reinforced our team's decision to configure automated CI/CD deployments via AWS Amplify for our Frontend right during our mid-term sprint.

#### 6. The Iceberg of Procrastination
* **Reflection & Takeaway:** This talk tackled the psychological roots of procrastination—a habit many students and developers fall into when facing overwhelming tasks or massive codebases. By exploring hidden triggers such as fear of failure and perfectionism, the presentation helped me reassess my time management workflow, teaching me how to break complex tasks down into manageable weekly milestones.

---

### Key Takeaways for My Career and Project

Attending the Saturday Meet up gave me more than just technical theory; it reshaped my engineering mindset:
* **Direct Application to Our Project:** Reinforced my understanding that great Frontend development isn't just about UI aesthetics—it requires thinking holistically about deployment automation (DevOps/CI/CD) and end-to-end user experience.
* **Professional Communication:** Realized that clear communication, technical confidence, and cross-functional collaboration are just as vital to project success as writing clean code.
* **Community Networking:** Connected directly with senior engineers and passionate peers across diverse universities, providing tremendous inspiration for my long-term career path in Cloud Computing.
